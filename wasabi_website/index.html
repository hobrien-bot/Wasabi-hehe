What Is Wasabi?

Wasabi is a green plant originally from Japan. It is most famous for being served with sushi and seafood dishes.

The spicy paste is made from the stem of the plant and creates a powerful heat that travels through the nose instead of burning the tongue like chilli peppers.

Real wasabi is extremely rare outside Japan because it is difficult and expensive to grow. Many restaurants use horseradish mixed with mustard and green colouring instead.

Why People Love Wasabi

People enjoy wasabi because it adds excitement and flavour to food. Its strong spicy effect disappears quickly, making it different from hot sauce.

Wasabi is commonly used with:

  • Sushi
  • Sashimi
  • Noodles
  • Rice dishes
  • Wasabi peas
  • Spicy sauces
