Ancient Japan

Wasabi has been used in Japan for over 1,000 years. The plant naturally grows near cool mountain streams.

Ancient Japanese communities discovered that wasabi improved the flavour of fish and helped reduce bacteria in raw seafood.

Because of this, wasabi became connected to sushi culture.

Traditional Farming

Growing real wasabi is difficult because it requires:

  • Cold flowing water
  • Shaded environments
  • Clean mountain conditions
  • Stable temperatures

Some plants take nearly two years before they are ready to harvest.

Wasabi Around The World

Today, wasabi is famous worldwide. Many countries now use wasabi flavouring in chips, snacks, nuts, sauces, and noodles.

Japanese restaurants helped spread the popularity of wasabi internationally.
